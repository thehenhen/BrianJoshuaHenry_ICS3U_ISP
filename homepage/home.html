What is this website?

Rather than re-inventing the wheel by creating yet another website that explains competitive programming concepts in detail, this website instead focuses on visualizing the concepts. For all the topics that are covered, we have interactive and visual demonstrations that will illustrate rather than explain them. Although we do not go deep into the details of exactly how the implementation of the code looks like, we did leave some links to articles and practice problems to ensure that you really understand the concepts well.

What lessons are covered in this website?

So far, this website covers six topics, not necessarily ranging from the easiest difficulty to the hardest. Each of these six data structures / algorithms are quite commonly applicable and anybody who is interested in competitive programming should have them under their belt. This website is also subject to expansion in the future if Google ever purchases it.

Why care about competitive programming?

If you're one of the people who are offended by the words "competitive programming", let us convince you in this paragraph why competitive programming, for what it's worth, is surprisingly interesting and useful. Firstly, it really helps you solidify your knowledge behind basic programming concepts, as it wires your brain to literally think like a computer. While you may already be familiar with all the functions and algorithms that you rely on when making a project, learning competitive programming actually analyzes how these functions work, and it teaches you to consider efficiency and accuracy. There's no reason not to discover all these interesting algorithms designed by computer scientists and learn how they work, while also practicing your problem-solving skills along the way.
